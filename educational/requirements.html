Actual Onboarding Requirements

The definitive checklist of physical documentation, technological infrastructure, and local card funding protocols required to launch successfully from PNG.

Requirement 01

Sovereign Identity Verification (KYC)

Because tier-1 international brokerages are bound by global anti-money laundering (AML) laws, you cannot open a trading profile anonymously or under a fake alias. You must upload high-resolution, uncropped digital scans of official government-issued identification assets to clear security checkpoints.

Acceptable PNG ID Instruments:

• Primary Choice: A valid Papua New Guinea National Identity (NID) Card or blue Sovereign Passport.

• Secondary Choice: A current PNG Land Transport Board (LTB) Driver's License.

• Proof of Address Document: A commercial bank transaction statement (BSP, Kina Bank, Westpac) or utility bill (PNG Power, Water PNG) generated within the last 90 days showing your exact physical residential location.

Requirement 02

International Card Protocols (Visa/Mastercard)

Standard local PNG commercial accounts operating solely on domestic Kina transactions cannot link to interbank trading networks. To route account funding and process withdrawals, you must hold a valid card capable of clearing cross-border e-commerce payments.

Your card name **must perfectly match** the name registered on your brokerage profile, or automated security firewalls will reject the deposit to prevent third-party transaction fraud.

  • • BSP Kundu Cards: Standard green domestic Kundu cards **will not work**. You must upgrade your account framework to a BSP Visa Debit Card.
  • • Kina Bank & Westpac Options: Active retail Visa/Mastercard options are fully functional provided online usage functions are unlocked by branch managers.
  • • Base Conversions: While your local account holds PGK, the card terminal will dynamically perform the institutional cross-rate conversion into standard reserve values (USD or AUD) upon funding.
Requirement 03

Stable Network Connectivity & Device Assets

Trading requires stable digital processing speeds. Sudden dropouts or structural lagging during volatile economic announcements can delay your server orders, triggering unexpected slippage expenses. Your personal hardware layout must match these tracking standards:

Internet Pipeline

A reliable 4G/LTE mobile data connection or fixed local broadband. Keep an extra data bundle active on a backup network (e.g., holding both Telikom and Digicel access points) to bypass unexpected telecom outages.

Primary Machine

A laptop or desktop computer running Windows or macOS. Desktop terminals are vital to perform rigorous multi-timeframe chart analysis, map support/resistance ceilings, and review long-term statistical trends cleanly.

Mobile Tracker

A smartphone running iOS or Android. Utilize mobile charting applications purely as an emergency monitoring terminal to adjust stop-loss boundaries or close open risk metrics when away from your primary desk.

Requirement 04

Minimum Starting Capital & Mental Discipline

While many international brokerages permit micro-deposits as low as $10 USD, attempting to navigate the markets under-capitalized can compromise your risk metrics. To properly implement our strict 1% risk mitigation formulas on micro contracts (0.01 lot size), a realistic initial capital baseline is **$50 USD to $100 USD** (approximately 200 to 400 Kina depending on active bank exchange margins).

CRITICAL WARNING: This capital must consist strictly of personal investment savings. Never trade using capital borrowed from family lines, high-interest local loan channels, or funds required for essential household living expenses.
