Phase 01

Prepare Your PNG Compliance Infrastructure

Before executing a single trade on an interbank terminal, you must assemble your physical compliance identification files. Offshore international clearing brokers follow rigorous **Know Your Customer (KYC)** laws. Because you are registering from Papua New Guinea, you must have clean, unexpired identity frameworks ready to pass automated compliance screening systems.

Required Documentation Checkpoints:
  • Primary ID: A valid PNG National Identity (NID) Card, a current PNG Driver’s License, or a PNG Sovereign Passport.
  • Proof of Residence: A formal bank statement from BSP, Kina Bank, or Westpac stamped within the last 3 months, or a formal company employment verification letter showing your physical residential address.

Configure Local Bank Pipelines & Funding Cards

You cannot fund trading environments using standard local corporate checks or basic domestic Kina accounts. Your clearing pipeline must use an international card transaction protocol. Request a **Visa Debit Card** or **Mastercard** directly linked to your personal savings structure. Phase 04

The Low-Capital Live Transition Loop

Once you graduate from the demo environment, your final onboarding milestone is transitioning into live market execution. Do not deposit massive savings blocks immediately out of greed. The massive psychological shift that occurs when risking real capital can disrupt your analytical discipline.

Execute micro lot positions (0.01 lot size parameters) where each pip movement equals approximately $0.10 USD. This path allows you to experience actual market speed variations, live spread costs, and real emotional variables while protecting your main financial stability.
