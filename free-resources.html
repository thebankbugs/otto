🌍 Introduction to Forex Trading

Welcome to your first lesson in the BANK BUGS Beginner Academy. Before you learn how to trade, it is important to understand what the Forex market is, who uses it, and why prices move every second around the world.

1. What is Forex?

Forex stands for Foreign Exchange. It is the global marketplace where one country's currency is exchanged for another.

Every day, banks, businesses, governments and individual traders exchange currencies for international trade, travel and investment.

The Forex market operates 24 hours a day from Monday to Friday and is the largest financial market in the world.

PNG Example

Imagine a business owner in Port Moresby importing machinery from Australia. They cannot pay using Papua New Guinea Kina (PGK). They first exchange PGK into Australian Dollars (AUD). That currency exchange happens through the Forex market.

2. Who Uses the Forex Market?

Many people think Forex is only for traders. In reality, millions of people and organisations participate every day.

  • 🏦 Commercial Banks
  • 🌍 Central Banks
  • 🏢 International Companies
  • 💰 Investment Funds
  • 👨‍💻 Retail Traders
  • 🌏 Governments

Retail traders simply try to identify opportunities created by these larger market participants.

3. Why Do Prices Move?

Currency prices constantly move because buyers and sellers enter and leave the market.

Some common reasons include:

  • 📈 Economic News
  • 💵 Interest Rates
  • 🏛 Government Policies
  • 🌎 Global Events
  • 📊 Supply and Demand
Remember

Nobody controls the Forex market. Prices move because millions of buyers and sellers make decisions every second.

4. What are Currency Pairs?

In Forex, currencies are always traded in pairs.

Examples include:

  • EUR/USD
  • GBP/USD
  • AUD/USD
  • USD/JPY

The first currency is called the Base Currency. The second is called the Quote Currency.

If you believe the first currency will become stronger, you buy the pair. If you believe it will become weaker, you sell the pair.

5. Trading Requires Education

Forex trading is not a get-rich-quick scheme. Professional traders spend years learning how markets work, managing risk and controlling their emotions.

At BANK BUGS, we encourage every beginner to:

  • ✅ Learn the basics first.
  • ✅ Open a Demo Account.
  • ✅ Practise before risking real money.
  • ✅ Never trade money you cannot afford to lose.

📌 Lesson Summary

  • ✔ Forex is the world's largest financial market.
  • ✔ Currencies are traded in pairs.
  • ✔ Prices move because of supply and demand.
  • ✔ Banks, companies and traders all participate.
  • ✔ Education and practice come before live trading.

After creating your broker profile, you'll usually be asked to verify your identity before accessing all account features. This process is called Know Your Customer (KYC). Verification is a standard part of opening accounts with regulated financial service providers. It helps protect both customers and the broker.

  • 🛡 Confirm customer identity

  • 🛡 Meet legal and regulatory obligations
  • 🛡 Improve account security
🇵🇬 PNG Example

Verification times vary. Some accounts are reviewed quickly, while others may take longer if additional information is needed. Submitting clear, complete documents can help reduce delays.

📊 Basic Charting Tools in MetaTrader 5 (MT5)

🎯 MISSION OBJECTIVE

By the end of this module, you will understand how to read MT5 charts, switch timeframes, and use basic tools to analyse price movement.

Charts are the core of trading. Every decision in Forex is based on what price is doing on the chart — not guesses, not emotions, and not predictions. MetaTrader 5 gives you a visual map of the market so you can understand buyer and seller behaviour in real time.

1. Understanding the MT5 Chart Screen

When you open a chart in MT5, you will usually see:

  • 📈 Candlesticks (price movement)
  • 📊 Timeframe controls
  • 📉 Buy/Sell price lines
  • ⚙️ Indicators (optional tools)
💡 BANK BUGS INTEL

Think of a chart like a live story of the market. Each candle shows how buyers and sellers fought over price during a specific time period.

2. Candlesticks Explained

Each candlestick represents price movement over a selected timeframe.

  • 🟢 Green Candle = Price went UP (buyers in control)
  • 🔴 Red Candle = Price went DOWN (sellers in control)

Each candle has 4 key points:

  • 🔼 High (highest price reached)
  • 🔽 Low (lowest price reached)
  • 🟢 Open (starting price)
  • 🔴 Close (ending price)
🇵🇬 PNG Example

Think of a candle like a daily negotiation between buyers and sellers. If buyers win, the candle closes green. If sellers win, it closes red.

3. Timeframes (VERY IMPORTANT)

Timeframes determine how much market data each candle represents.

  • ⏱ M1 = 1 minute chart (fast movement)
  • ⏱ M5 = 5 minute chart
  • ⏱ M15 = 15 minute chart
  • ⏱ H1 = 1 hour chart
  • ⏱ H4 = 4 hour chart
  • ⏱ D1 = Daily chart (big picture)

Lower timeframes = noise and speed. Higher timeframes = structure and direction.

4. Basic Chart Tools in MT5

💡 BANK BUGS INTEL

5. Beginner Chart Setup (Recommended)

  • ✔ Use candlestick chart (not line chart)

  • ✔ Remove unnecessary indicators
  • ✔ Keep your chart clean and simple
⚠ FIELD WARNING

Always check the following before executing any trade:
