🌍 Introduction to Forex Trading

Welcome to your first lesson in the BANK BUGS Beginner Academy. Before you learn how to trade, it is important to understand what the Forex market is, who uses it, and why prices move every second around the world.

1. What is Forex?

Forex stands for Foreign Exchange. It is the global marketplace where one country's currency is exchanged for another.

Every day, banks, businesses, governments and individual traders exchange currencies for international trade, travel and investment.

The Forex market operates 24 hours a day from Monday to Friday and is the largest financial market in the world.

PNG Example

Imagine a business owner in Port Moresby importing machinery from Australia. They cannot pay using Papua New Guinea Kina (PGK). They first exchange PGK into Australian Dollars (AUD). That currency exchange happens through the Forex market.

2. Who Uses the Forex Market?

Many people think Forex is only for traders. In reality, millions of people and organisations participate every day.

  • 🏦 Commercial Banks
  • 🌍 Central Banks
  • 🏢 International Companies
  • 💰 Investment Funds
  • 👨‍💻 Retail Traders
  • 🌏 Governments

Retail traders simply try to identify opportunities created by these larger market participants.

3. Why Do Prices Move?

Currency prices constantly move because buyers and sellers enter and leave the market.

Some common reasons include:

  • 📈 Economic News
  • 💵 Interest Rates
  • 🏛 Government Policies
  • 🌎 Global Events
  • 📊 Supply and Demand
Remember

Nobody controls the Forex market. Prices move because millions of buyers and sellers make decisions every second.

4. What are Currency Pairs?

In Forex, currencies are always traded in pairs.

Examples include:

  • EUR/USD
  • GBP/USD
  • AUD/USD
  • USD/JPY

The first currency is called the Base Currency. The second is called the Quote Currency.

If you believe the first currency will become stronger, you buy the pair. If you believe it will become weaker, you sell the pair.

5. Trading Requires Education

Forex trading is not a get-rich-quick scheme. Professional traders spend years learning how markets work, managing risk and controlling their emotions.

At BANK BUGS, we encourage every beginner to:

  • ✅ Learn the basics first.
  • ✅ Open a Demo Account.
  • ✅ Practise before risking real money.
  • ✅ Never trade money you cannot afford to lose.

📌 Lesson Summary

  • ✔ Forex is the world's largest financial market.
  • ✔ Currencies are traded in pairs.
  • ✔ Prices move because of supply and demand.
  • ✔ Banks, companies and traders all participate.
  • ✔ Education and practice come before live trading.

🖥️ Trading Platforms (MetaTrader 5)

Now that you understand what the Forex market is, the next step is learning how traders access it. A trading platform is the software that connects you to your broker and allows you to view live market prices, analyse charts and place trades from your mobile phone or computer.

1. What is a Trading Platform?

A trading platform is an application used to access the financial markets through your broker. Think of it as the control centre where you can monitor prices, open trades, manage your account and review your trading history.

Without a trading platform, you cannot buy or sell currencies in the Forex market.

🇵🇬 PNG Example

Think of a trading platform like the BSP Mobile Banking App. You use the app to check your account balance, transfer money and pay bills. In the same way, traders use a trading platform to view market prices, analyse charts and place trades.

2. What is MetaTrader 5 (MT5)?

MetaTrader 5, commonly known as MT5, is one of the world's most popular trading platforms. Millions of traders use it every day to trade Forex, commodities, indices and other financial markets.

Most international brokers, including our preferred broker, support MT5 because it is fast, reliable and packed with professional trading tools.

  • 📈 Live Market Charts
  • 📊 Multiple Timeframes
  • 📉 Technical Indicators
  • ⚡ Fast Trade Execution
  • 📱 Mobile & Desktop Versions

3. What Can You Do Inside MT5?

MT5 provides everything you need to manage your trading account.

  • ✔ View live Forex prices
  • ✔ Open and close trades
  • ✔ Analyse candlestick charts
  • ✔ Set Stop Loss and Take Profit levels

  • ✔ Monitor profits and losses in real time

As you progress through this course, you will learn how to use each of these features step by step.

MetaTrader 5 is completely free to download. Your broker will provide you with a trading account that you can use to log in and access the platform.

You can install MT5 on:

🏢 Local & International Brokers

Before you can begin trading, you need a broker. A broker acts as the bridge between you and the global financial markets. In this lesson, you'll learn the difference between local banks, local financial institutions, and international Forex brokers.

1. What is a Broker?

A broker is a licensed financial company that gives traders access to buy and sell financial products such as currencies, commodities, indices and other markets.

Think of a broker as the gateway that connects your trading platform to the global Forex market.

🇵🇬 PNG Example

Imagine you want to buy a product from another country. You cannot simply walk into the overseas warehouse. Instead, you use a shipping company to deliver it to Papua New Guinea. In trading, the broker performs a similar role by connecting your trading orders to the financial markets.

2. Can I Trade Forex Through My Local Bank?

Most banks in Papua New Guinea provide everyday banking services such as savings accounts, loans, debit cards and international money transfers.

They generally do not provide retail Forex trading accounts through platforms like MetaTrader 5.

This is why many retail traders use international brokers that specialise in online trading services.

  • 🏦 BSP
  • 🏦 Kina Bank
  • 🏦 Westpac PNG

These institutions are important for banking and moving funds, while an international broker provides access to trading platforms and market execution.

3. Why Do Traders Use International Brokers?

International brokers specialise in providing access to global financial markets.

Many offer:

  • ✔ MetaTrader 5 access
  • ✔ Demo trading accounts
  • ✔ Competitive spreads
  • ✔ Fast trade execution
  • ✔ Educational resources
  • ✔ Customer support

Choosing a broker carefully is important because you are trusting them with your trading account and funds.

4. What Should You Look For?

Before opening an account with any broker, consider the following:

  • 🔒 Appropriate regulation and licensing.
  • 📱 Support for MetaTrader 5.
  • 💵 Transparent fees and spreads.
  • ⚡ Reliable deposits and withdrawals.
  • 📞 Responsive customer support.
  • 📚 Educational tools for beginners.

🔍 Broker Verification (KYC)

🎯 MISSION OBJECTIVE

Learn why brokers verify customer identities, what "Know Your Customer (KYC)" means, and how to complete the process smoothly.

After creating your broker profile, you'll usually be asked to verify your identity before accessing all account features. This process is called Know Your Customer (KYC). KYC helps financial companies confirm that customers are who they say they are and supports fraud prevention and financial security.

1. Why Do Brokers Verify Customers?

Verification is a standard part of opening accounts with regulated financial service providers. It helps protect both customers and the broker.

  • 🛡 Confirm customer identity
  • 🛡 Help prevent fraud
  • 🛡 Meet legal and regulatory obligations
  • 🛡 Improve account security
🇵🇬 PNG Example

When opening a bank account in Papua New Guinea, you're commonly asked to provide identification before the account is activated. Many online brokers follow a similar verification process.

2. What Happens During Verification?

The exact steps vary between brokers, but you may be asked to:

  • 📤 Upload your identification document.
  • 📤 Upload proof of address (if requested).
  • 🤳 Complete a selfie or identity check.
  • 📧 Confirm your email address.
  • 📱 Verify your mobile number.

Follow the broker's instructions carefully and make sure all information is accurate.

3. How Long Does Verification Take?

Verification times vary. Some accounts are reviewed quickly, while others may take longer if additional information is needed. Submitting clear, complete documents can help reduce delays.

💡 BANK BUGS INTEL

Before uploading your documents, check that your photos are sharp, well-lit and show the entire document. Clear uploads can help avoid unnecessary requests for resubmission.

4. Common Mistakes

  • ❌ Uploading blurry photos.
  • ❌ Using expired identification.
  • ❌ Entering information that doesn't match your ID.
  • ❌ Cropping the document so important details aren't visible.
  • ❌ Submitting documents in unsupported file formats.
⚠ FIELD WARNING

Only upload your documents through the broker's official website or app. Protect your personal information and never send sensitive documents to unknown individuals or unofficial channels.

5. Verification Complete... What's Next?

Once your account has been verified, you're ready to create your first Demo Trading Account and connect it to MetaTrader 5. That's exactly what you'll learn in the next module.
